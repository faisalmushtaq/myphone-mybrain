About the Project

The MyPhone/MyBrain project is a research initiative investigating how everyday smartphone use influences memory, attention, decision-making, and overall mental health. By combining large-scale passive sensing with validated cognitive assessments, we aim to reveal nuanced patterns in the relationship between digital device use and brain function.

Our work spans multiple disciplines — psychology, neuroscience, data science, and human-computer interaction — to build a comprehensive picture of how modern technology shapes the mind.

🧠

Cognitive Assessment

Validated tasks measuring memory, attention, and executive function delivered via smartphone.

📱

Passive Sensing

Unobtrusive collection of usage patterns, app categories, and screen-time metrics.

📊

Data-Driven Insights

Machine-learning models linking behavioural signals to cognitive and well-being outcomes.

🔒

Privacy First

Strict ethical oversight and privacy-preserving methods protect participant data throughout.

Research Areas

Our interdisciplinary team investigates several interconnected themes:

🔍

Attention & Distraction

How notifications and multitasking affect sustained attention and focus.

💾

Memory Offloading

The extent to which reliance on smartphones alters encoding and recall strategies.

😴

Sleep & Well-being

Links between evening screen use, sleep quality, and next-day cognitive performance.

🤝

Social Cognition

How social-media engagement shapes empathy, social comparison, and self-perception.
